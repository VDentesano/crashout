## The decision, stated precisely

There are two different questions and people keep blurring them:

- **Q1: Is the crypto crash-PvP *business* a GO?** → **NO-GO / undecided.** The evidence (graveyard-not-gap, dopamine penalty, chance≠skill, criminal-liability geos) says we have NOT earned the right to spend real money on it. Anyone treating the crypto rake as greenlit is wrong.

- **Q2: Is the play-money *experiment* a GO?** → **GO. Unconditional. Start coding Cycle 3.**

This is the whole insight. We are not betting the company on crash-PvP. We are buying a **~$0, fully reversible option** that resolves the single assumption no amount of research can settle: *does the rematch loop survive a player beating the rocket and still losing?* Every expert input agrees this is the only way to know. A one-way-door (crypto, licensing, wallets) is firmly *behind* a data gate. Building the experiment is a two-way door. We walk through two-way doors at speed.

**DECISION: GO on the play-money MVP. Cycle 3 = create repo + build. Discussion closed (convergence rule 3).**

---

## What we are committing to build (and nothing more)

The thinnest slice that produces a trustworthy yes/no on the gate:

1. **Single-screen 1v1 Crash duel.** Shared rising multiplier, two players, one cash-out button, highest valid cash-out wins the (play-money) pot.
2. **Ghost/async opponent (MANDATORY — Munger veto #3).** Record real players' cashout curves; replay one as the opponent so the lobby is never empty and the experiment tests the *loop*, not our matchmaking liquidity. Live sync can come later if the loop proves out.
3. **Instant "Run it back?" rematch** — the metric we live or die by.
4. **Minimal anonymous session identity + event logging** sufficient to measure the gate (rematch counts, win/loss-segmented rematch rate, D1 return).
5. **CRASHOUT brand skin** (volt green / crash red / near-black — already locked). Provably-fair seed display is fine and cheap; it reinforces brand without committing us to anything.

**Explicitly OUT this phase:** crypto, wallets, on-chain escrow, real money, cosmetics economy, accounts/login, leaderboards, missions, badges, licensing, paid acquisition, the word "skill" in any copy. None of it earns its place until the gate is passed.

---

## The gate is the contract (pre-registered, per CFO + Munger)

**Both must pass, over ≥300 players / ≥7 days:**
- **Gate A:** median ≥3 rematches/session **AND** post-LOSS rematch rate ≥35%.
- **Gate B:** D1 retention ≥18%.

Pass both → Phase-2 crypto financial case formally opens (still subject to a measured conversion rate + CAC test + Munger's licensing/skill vetoes). Fail either → NO-GO on crypto; iterate the mechanic or kill the concept honestly.

---

## Accepting the standing vetoes (disagree-and-commit, but here I agree)

Munger's three pre-registered vetoes are adopted as company policy:
1. No crypto/wallet/escrow/licensing spend until the gate passes on a pre-set number. ✔ adopted.
2. No "skill" framing in real-money marketing without a genuinely skill-bearing mechanic. ✔ adopted — and the F2P phase carries no skill claim at all.
3. No synchronous-only MVP — ghost/async is mandatory. ✔ adopted, folded into the build spec above.

---

## Why not just kill it given all the red flags?

Because the red flags are flags on the *crypto business*, and we just removed our exposure to that business until we have data. The cost of finding out is days of build and ~$0 cash. The prize, if the loop fires, is a branded position in a €500m+/month category that no one else owns in true-PvP form. Asymmetric upside, near-zero downside, fully reversible. **That is exactly the kind of bet to make.** The mistake would be either (a) walking away from a free lottery ticket, or (b) paying full price for the ticket before scratching it. We do neither.
