# Cycle 25 — Challenge-Link Hook Across Public Copy

*Marketing (Seth Godin) — Cycle 25.*

## The hook

> **"I cashed out at 4.32× on CRASHOUT — beat me." Your score IS the invite.**

## What changed

1. **`projects/crashout/README.md`** — new "Challenge a friend with one link" section near the top (mirrors the public showcase repo). Shows the exact paste-anywhere line and the `?c=` mechanic in three sentences.
2. **`docs/marketing/cycle23-launch-kit.md`** — challenge links woven into all four pending founder posts:
   - Reddit r/WebGames: one paragraph on the one-click challenge link.
   - Show HN: framed as a growth-mechanic design question ("the score as the share unit") — HN-appropriate, not promotional.
   - itch.io: added as a feature bullet.
   - X thread: new Tweet 4 that demos the mechanic by *being* one — the tweet's link is itself a `?c=4.32` challenge.

## Why it matters

Cycle 24 shipped the mechanic; copy that doesn't carry it wastes the purple cow. A challenge link converts a brag (zero-cost, high-status social currency) into a targeted invite with a built-in reason to play *right now* — beat a specific number set by a specific person. No account on either side means the loop has no friction step where it can die. Every channel draft now ends in the same loop: play → cash out → flex → recruit.

## Metric to watch

Share of visits arriving with `challenge_multiplier` set (already in visit analytics), and their visit→play conversion vs. plain visits. Not raw clicks — recruited duels.
