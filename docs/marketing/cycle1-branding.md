# Cycle 1 — Brand for a 1v1 Crash PVP Crypto Duel

*Marketing (Seth Godin thinking) — the only marketing that works is a product worth talking about. Below, the product IS the marketing.*

---

## The audience (be specific)

Not "crypto gamblers." Our smallest viable audience: **young, competitive crypto-natives (men, 18–30, LATAM/SEA/Eastern Europe) who already play Aviator/crash games but are bored of betting against a faceless house.** They want a *human to beat*, a name on a leaderboard, and a clip to drop in the group chat. We give them an opponent, not a slot machine.

The status change we sell: **from "I gambled" to "I won a duel."** Skill story, not luck story.

## 1. Candidate names

## 2. Top 3, ranked

**#1 — Crashout**
**#2 — Versus**
**#3 — Klash**

Crashout wins because it does three jobs at once: it contains the category keyword (SEO + instant comprehension), it names the *action* (cash OUT), and it rides existing slang ("crashout" = going all-in) so it sounds native to the audience instead of invented by a marketing team. Versus is the safest and clearest, but "safe" is the risk Godin warns against — it's a great descriptor, a weak tribe-flag. Klash is the most brandable but loses the built-in meaning Crashout carries for free.

## 3. Brand build-out for #1: CRASHOUT

**Tagline:** *Cash out. Or crash out.*
(Two outcomes, one button, one opponent. Says the whole game in four words.)

**Positioning line:**
*"Crashout is the only crash game where you're not playing the house — you're hunting a real person who can feel you win."*

**Tone of voice (3 words):** *Reckless. Direct. Alive.*

**Color / identity direction:**
- **Volt green (#00FF85)** — the multiplier line climbing; "go," money, momentum.
- **Crash red / molten orange (#FF3B30)** — the detonation; the moment it blows.
- **Near-black (#0A0A0F)** — neon-on-dark arcade canvas; makes the line glow.

Feeling: a dark fight-night arena where two glowing lines race upward and one explodes. Not a casino's gold-and-velvet — a **competitive e-sport**. The whole UI is the green line vs. the red threat. Logo: "CRASHOUT" with the **O** as a circular gauge/explosion ring.

---

## 4. The Purple Cow (why they tell friends)

**You can SEE your opponent's nerve in real time — and the loser's screen literally crashes for them.**

In every other crash game you're alone with a rising number. In Crashout, the screen is split: **you watch the other human's cursor hover over their cash-out button, hesitating, while the line climbs.** You feel them sweat. When one player cashes out, the other gets a half-second of "do I risk it?" — and if they greed and crash, their screen **shatters/detonates** with their name on it.

That shareable moment — *the clip of you holding 0.3s longer than someone and watching their screen blow up* — is the thing that gets dropped in the group chat. The taunt-and-rematch loop is built in: every duel ends with a **"Run it back?"** button. We don't buy distribution; the humiliation-and-revenge clip IS the distribution.

> Remarkable test: would a player screen-record this and send it unprompted? **Yes — beating a *named human* and watching them detonate is a flex. Beating a house algorithm is not.**

## Channels (priority order — no ad budget needed)
1. **Word of mouth** — the rematch/clip loop above (built into the product).
2. **Build in Public** — dev posting duel clips on X/TikTok daily during build.
3. **Tribe seeding** — drop into existing Aviator/crash Telegram & Discord communities as the most helpful person, not a spammer.
4. **Email/Telegram list** — permission asset: "Duel of the Week" biggest-multiplier clips.
5. Paid ads = last resort, only to pour fuel on a proven word-of-mouth fire.

**Metrics that matter (not vanity):** rematch rate (% of duels → "Run it back"), clips shared per 100 duels, K-factor (invites per player). Ignore raw signups.
