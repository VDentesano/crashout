# Cycle 2 — Pre-Mortem & Inversion: CRASHOUT

_Munger thinking. Invert, always invert. I cannot delay, only veto. My job is to find the ways we die before we spend money dying._

**Date:** 2026-06-11 | **Subject:** The play-money-first → crypto-Phase-2 plan (Concept B, re-shaped per research)

---

## The question I actually answer

Not "is the crypto crash-PvP business good?" — the research already showed that bet is loaded with structural risk (graveyard, not gap). The question is narrower and it's the only one on the table this cycle:

**"Should we spend a few days of build to run a near-zero-cost play-money experiment that resolves the one make-or-break assumption no external evidence can settle?"**

Frame it that way and most of my objections evaporate — because the play-money MVP is not the business, it's a **cheap option on the business.** I judge the option, not the dream.

---

## Inversion: imagine it's 12 months out and CRASHOUT is dead. How did we kill it?

1. **We fell in love with the crypto rake and built it anyway.** We skipped the retention gate, shipped wallets and on-chain escrow, took on Anjouan licensing (~$15–20k), and *then* discovered the rematch loop doesn't survive the zero-sum dopamine penalty. We spent real money and criminal-liability exposure to learn something a $0 play-money test would have told us. **This is the #1 way we die. The plan as sequenced (play-money first) prevents it — so long as nobody jumps the gate.**

2. **The play-money test was rigged to pass.** We declared victory on a vanity metric ("look, people clicked rematch!") instead of a pre-registered bar. Self-delusion is the cheapest poison. **Mitigation: the gate must be written in blood BEFORE we see data** — median ≥3 rematches/session AND a D1 retention bar, set now, not negotiated after we're emotionally invested. CFO owns the exact D1 number.

3. **The zero-sum dopamine penalty is real and we ignored the signal.** A €160bn category that "forgot" to build matched-stake 1v1 crash did not forget. "I beat the rocket and STILL lost" is a worse feeling than house-crash where every rocket-beater wins. If our own test shows weak rematch behavior, that's not a bug to patch — it's the market telling us the truth. **The plan's value is precisely that it surfaces this for ~$0.**

4. **We marketed a chance game as a skill game and a regulator (or a plaintiff) made us pay.** Crash is P(cash@x)=1/x — pure chance. The "skill duel softens regulation" wedge that works for Skillz/BAAS does **not** transfer to raw crash. If we lean on the skill narrative in the real-money phase without injecting genuine skill into the mechanic, we are a litigation magnet wearing a costume. **Mitigation: in the F2P phase the skill claim buys nothing and risks nothing (no consideration → not gambling regardless). Defer the skill-vs-gambling fork to the Phase-2 gate. Do NOT put "skill" in any play-money marketing copy.**

5. **Synchronous matchmaking gave us an empty lobby and the product looked dead on arrival** — even in play-money. Nobody waits 60s for a free duel either. **Mitigation (must be in the MVP, not Phase 2): async/"ghost" duels — record a real player's cashout curve and replay it as the opponent.** This also de-risks the whole liquidity question that kills Concepts A and C. If we build synchronous-only, we've built the hard mode of the hard mode for no reason.

6. **We over-built the option.** Spent three weeks gold-plating cosmetics, accounts, leaderboards, and an i18n pipeline on an experiment whose only job is to answer one yes/no question. **Mitigation: the MVP is single-screen, ghost-opponent, rematch button, one retention metric. Nothing else earns its place until the gate is passed.**

---

## What I am NOT worried about (so we don't waste a veto)

- **Infra cost.** INSFORGE + Cloudflare free tiers carry a play-money experiment to thousands of DAU at ~$0. There is no money-spend trigger here, so no escalation needed to build it.
- **Brand risk.** CRASHOUT play-money is a game, not a gambling product. Shipping it commits us to nothing regulatory.
- **"Is the TAM real?"** Research settled this — the category is enormous. Our risk was never demand for *crash*; it's demand for *our PvP variant*. Only the test answers it.

---

## Verdict

**NO VETO on building the play-money MVP.** It is a cheap, reversible, information-generating option, sequenced to put every fatal risk *behind* a data gate. Building it is the rational move; *not* building it (and either guessing or jumping straight to crypto) is the irrational one.

**STANDING VETO, pre-registered, on three things:**

1. **VETO any crypto/wallet/escrow/licensing spend until the retention gate is passed with a number set in advance.** No "let's just add stakes to see." The gate is the gate.
2. **VETO any use of "skill" / "skill duel" framing in real-money marketing unless a genuinely skill-bearing mechanic is added first.** Market chance as chance. Get the gambling license if it comes to that; don't litigate our way out of a costume.
3. **VETO a synchronous-only MVP.** Ghost/async opponent is mandatory, or the experiment fails for a logistics reason instead of telling us the truth about the loop.

**One sentence:** *Build the cheap experiment, write the pass/fail line before you look, and don't let anyone spend a dollar on crypto until the line is crossed.*
